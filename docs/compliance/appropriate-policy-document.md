# Appropriate Policy Document (APD)

**For processing of special-category and criminal-offence data**
*Required by Schedule 1, Part 4 of the Data Protection Act 2018.*

> Template — each clinic adapts and retains its own copy. Wellspring maintains this APD
> for its own processing as a processor; the clinic (controller) maintains an equivalent.

## 1. Description of processing
Wellspring processes **health data** (special category under Art. 9 UK GDPR) contained
in clinician session notes and patient self-reported wellbeing check-ins, on behalf of
clinics, to provide wellbeing and practice-administration support.

## 2. Schedule 1 condition relied upon
Processing is carried out under the controller's lawful basis. The typical conditions
are:
- **Art. 9(2)(h)** — provision of health/social care (clinic-side), and/or
- **Art. 9(2)(a)** — explicit consent (patient-facing wellbeing companion; results flow
  to the clinician **only** with explicit patient consent).

The corresponding DPA 2018 Schedule 1 conditions are documented per clinic.

## 3. Compliance with the data protection principles
- **Lawfulness, fairness, transparency:** privacy information provided at intake; consent
  captured and withdrawable.
- **Purpose limitation:** data used only for wellbeing/administration; no secondary use;
  no AI training on clinic data.
- **Data minimisation:** only data the clinician/patient enters; no clinical scoring.
- **Accuracy:** clinicians review AI-drafted summaries before use.
- **Storage limitation:** configurable retention and deletion per clinic.
- **Integrity & confidentiality:** RLS isolation, encryption, audit logging, RBAC.
- **Accountability:** audit trail, DPIA, this APD, and the DPA.

## 4. Retention and erasure
Default retention is configurable per clinic. On expiry or on a valid erasure request,
records are deleted (or returned) using the export/deletion tooling, and the action is
logged.

## 5. Review
This document is reviewed at least annually and on any material change to processing.
